1. Project Drivers 
 
1.1 Purpose of the Project 
The purpose of this project is to specify and develop a Healthcare Management System for a 
medium-sized hospital or clinic. The system supports the end-to-end lifecycle of outpatient care, 
including patient registration, schedule and appointment management, clinical documentation, 
billing and payments, reminders, and operational reporting. 
This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) defines the functional and non-functional 
requirements of the Healthcare Management System. It is based on the V olere Requirements 
Specification Template v16 and the analytical work from Deliverable 2 (Noun Technique, class 
diagram, CRUD analysis, state machines, activity diagrams, and System Sequence Diagrams). 
The SRS serves as a contract between stakeholders (teaching staff, student team, and the notional 
hospital client) and the development team, and as a reference for design, implementation, testing, 
and grading. 
 
1.2 User Business / Background 
 
The target environment is a hospital or outpatient clinic that manages patient care using a 
combination of manual processes and fragmented software. Core activities such as registering 
patients, booking and cancelling appointments, completing treatments, generating bills, sending 
reminders, and producing management reports are often handled in separate spreadsheets, paper 
forms, or ad hoc tools. This leads to: 
 Scheduling conflicts and double-booked resources. 
 Missed or late appointments due to lack of reminders. 
 Delayed or inconsistent billing and payment tracking. 
 Incomplete medical records and weak audit trails. 
 Limited visibility into operational performance (e.g., appointment utilization, overdue 
bills). 
The Healthcare Management System is intended as an integrated solution that: 


===== PAGE 4 =====

 Centralizes core information about Patients, Doctors, Appointments, MedicalRecords, 
Prescriptions, Rooms, and Bills (as identified by the Noun Technique and class diagram). 
 Coordinates the work of Patients, Receptionists, Doctors, Administrators, and automated 
schedulers around a consistent domain model. 
 Automates recurring and time-dependent behaviours captured in the state machines, 
activity diagrams, and SSDs (e.g., appointment reminders, cleanup of expired slots, 
monthly billing). 
 Provides a realistic case study for applying requirements engineering techniques (V olere 
template, atomic requirements with fit criteria, UML modeling, CRUD analysis) within 
the C-SW312 course. 
1.3 Goals of the Project 
G1 – Improve appointment and schedule management 
The system shall provide reliable booking, confirmation, and cancellation workflows that 
prevent double-booking and enforce the hospital’s cancellation policy. 
 Advantage: Fewer conflicts and no-shows; more efficient use of doctors and rooms. 
 Measurement: For all modeled scenarios (UC-01 to UC-12 and UC-T1–T3), 
appointments respect doctor and room availability, no overlapping confirmed 
appointments exist for the same doctor-room-timeslot combination, and the Appointment 
state machine transitions match the defined lifecycle (Requested, Active, Confirmed, 
InProgress, Completed, Cancelled, Expired). 
G2 – Support accurate billing and payment handling 
The system shall generate and manage Bills for completed treatments and support robust 
payment workflows, including failure and retry handling. 
 Advantage: Clear financial records per patient, alignment between clinical activity and 
billing, and realistic integration with external payment services. 
 Measurement: For the test scenarios, every completed treatment with billable items 
results in at least one corresponding Bill; each Bill progresses through the Bill lifecycle 
states (Created, PendingPayment, Paid, Overdue, WrittenOff) according to the specified 
business rules and use cases (UC-05, UC-07, UC-11, UC-T1). 
G3 – Enhance information availability and auditability 
The system shall maintain a single, consistent MedicalRecord per patient and trace key 
operations across use cases, requirements, and domain classes. 
 Advantage: Safer clinical decision making, less duplication, and strong justification for 
design choices across Deliverables 2 and 3.

2. Project Constraints 
2.1 Mandated Constraints (technology, deadlines, budget) 
C-01 – Core domain data must be persistent 
The system shall persist all core domain concepts – Patient, Doctor, Appointment, 
MedicalRecord, Prescription, Room, Bill – in a durable data store so that clinical and financial 
history can be retrieved over time. 
C-02 – No physical deletion of MedicalRecord data 
The system shall not physically delete MedicalRecord data. Logical deactivation or archival is 
allowed only through defined maintenance or administrative procedures, and historical clinical 
information must remain accessible for audit and safety. 
C-03 – Controlled deletion of other entities via maintenance use cases 
Deletion or cleanup of Appointments, slots, and related transient data shall be performed via 
explicit maintenance use cases (e.g., Clean up expired slots) rather than ad hoc bulk deletion, and 
shall preserve an audit trail. 


===== PAGE 8 =====

C-04 – External payment gateway integration 
All electronic payments shall be handled through an external secure Payment Gateway. The 
Healthcare Management System shall not store full payment card details, but only non-sensitive 
identifiers and status returned by the gateway. 
C-05 – Course deadline constraint 
The SRS, diagrams, and any prototype produced from it shall be completed within the schedule 
defined for the C-SW312 course deliverables. Scope and level of detail must be achievable 
within this deadline while still satisfying the functional and non-functional requirements 
documented here. 
C-06 – Educational budget constraint 
The project is conducted as a course assignment with no real monetary budget. The solution 
must assume the use of standard open-source or institutionally provided tools and platforms, 
without dependence on expensive commercial licenses beyond what is already available in the 
teaching environment. 
 
 
 
 
2.2 Relevant Facts and Assumptions 
The following facts and high-level assumptions shape the requirements and design of the 
Healthcare Management System: 
 Each patient is uniquely identified within the system (e.g., by patientID). 
 There is exactly one long-lived MedicalRecord per patient, aggregating all consultations 
and clinical notes. 
 An Appointment links exactly one Patient and one Doctor at a specific date and 
time, and may reserve a Room. 
 Bills are generated from completed treatments and track payment status, overdue 
handling, and possible write-offs. 
 External services (Payment Gateway, NotificationService, ReportingService, 
ExternalPatientSystem) provide stable APIs for integration. 
 More detailed assumptions and dependencies are described in Section 7 of this SRS.

2.3 Implementation Environment and Partner Systems 
 
The implementation environment describes the technical context in which the Healthcare 
Management System operates within a medium-sized hospital or clinic. The system assumes: 
• A persistent data store (e.g., a relational database) able to store core domain classes such 
as Patient, Doctor, Appointment, MedicalRecord, Prescription, Room, and Bill. 
• One or more application servers hosting the business logic and APIs that implement the 
functional and non-functional requirements in this SRS. 
• User access via standard desktop or web clients for receptionists, doctors, administrators, 
finance staff, and IT operations. 
 
 The system collaborates with the following external or partner applications at the system 
boundary: 
• Payment Gateway: Processes electronic payments for bills initiated from the “Make 
payment” use case and returns success or failure responses that drive the Bill lifecycle 
(PendingPayment, Paid, Overdue, WrittenOff). 
• NotificationService: Delivers SMS/email notifications for reminders, confirmations, and 
cancellations, and reports delivery success or failure for logging and summary reporting. 
• ReportingService: Generates weekly and monthly operational reports requested by the 
system scheduler and returns summaries or report artifacts. 
• ExternalPatientSystem: Supplies or receives patient demographic and clinical data when 
synchronizing patient information and is invoked by the “Synchronize patient 
information” use case and related SSD. 
 
These partner systems are treated as black boxes; only their interfaces and observable behaviours 
(as shown in the SSDs) are relevant.

3. Functional Requirements 
 
3.1 Scope of the Work 
The scope of the work covers the operational processes involved in managing outpatient care 
and related administrative tasks at a hospital or clinic. The following business activities are in 
scope: 
 Registering and maintaining patient information. 
 Managing doctor schedules and appointment slots. 
 Booking, confirming, cancelling, and completing appointments. 
 Recording treatments and updating patient MedicalRecords. 


===== PAGE 11 =====

 Generating and managing bills for completed treatments. 
 Handling patient payments, including payment failures and retries. 
 Sending appointment reminders and related notifications. 
 Generating weekly and monthly operational reports. 
 Running maintenance and cleanup jobs for expired or inconsistent data. 
 Synchronizing patient information with an external system when required. 
These activities are captured in the use cases defined in Deliverable 2, including UC-01 to 
UC-12 and UC-T1 to UC-T3. 
 
3.2 Scope of the Product 
The Healthcare Management System is responsible for automating the above work within a 
defined system boundary. The product: 
 Maintains the core domain objects: Patient, Doctor, Appointment, MedicalRecord, 
Prescription, Room, Bill. 
 Provides user interfaces and APIs for Patients, Receptionists, Doctors, Administrators, 
and Finance/Billing staff. 
 Communicates with external services (Payment Gateway, NotificationService, 
ReportingService, ExternalPatientSystem) through defined interfaces. 
 Manages state transitions for Appointments and Bills as specified by the state machine 
diagrams. 
 Executes scheduled jobs for reminders, reporting, billing, and cleanup. 
The product explicitly does not cover low-level hardware details, detailed UI screen design, or 
deep analytics beyond the reports described in the functional requirements. 
 
3.3 Functional Requirements  
FR-01 – Register new patient 
 Description: The system shall allow a receptionist to register a new patient by 
entering core demographic and contact details. 
 Source: UC-02 Register patient information; Noun Technique (Patient). 


===== PAGE 12 =====

 Rationale: New patients must be captured before any clinical or billing activities can 
take place. 
 Fit Criterion: Given a receptionist with access rights, when they complete all mandatory 
registration fields and submit, the system creates a new Patient record with a unique 
patientID and displays a confirmation. 
FR-02 – Maintain patient information 
 Description: The system shall allow authorized users to update existing patient 
information (e.g., address, phone, insurance details) while preserving historical data 
where required. 
 Source: UC-02 Register patient information; UC-T3 Synchronize patient information. 
 Rationale: Patient data frequently changes and must remain accurate. 
 Fit Criterion: Given an existing patient, when an authorized user edits and saves 
changes, the updated fields are stored and visible in subsequent views of the patient 
profile. 
FR-03 – Create and update medical record 
 Description: The system shall automatically create a MedicalRecord for each new 
patient and allow doctors to update it with diagnoses, allergies, and clinical notes after 
each consultation. 
 Source: UC-02 Register patient information; UC-05 Complete patient treatments. 
 Rationale: A single, longitudinal medical record per patient is required for safe and 
consistent care. 
 Fit Criterion: For each patient, exactly one MedicalRecord exists; after a completed 
treatment, new clinical notes are appended and visible in the patient’s history. 
FR-04 – Manage doctor schedules 
 Description: The system shall allow a doctor or authorized staff to view, add, modify, 
and block timeslots in a doctor’s schedule. 
 Source: UC-03 Manage doctor schedule. 
 Rationale: Controlled schedule management is needed to avoid conflicts and support 
booking. 
 Fit Criterion: When a doctor submits schedule changes with no conflicts, the updated 
slots are stored and are reflected in subsequent appointment searches. 
FR-05 – Book appointment 


===== PAGE 13 =====

 Description: The system shall allow a patient to book an appointment with a doctor by 
selecting a date, time, and available slot. 
 Source: UC-01 Book appointment. 
 Rationale: Booking appointments is a core service of the hospital system. 
 Fit Criterion: Given an available slot and a valid patient, when booking is requested, an 
Appointment with status Requested or Confirmed is created and linked to the Patient and 
Doctor. 
FR-06 – Confirm appointment slots 
 Description: The system shall allow a receptionist or authorized staff to confirm 
pending appointments and assign them to a final room and timeslot. 
 Source: UC-04 Confirm appointment slot. 
 Rationale: Some organizations require manual confirmation before appointments 
become final. 
 Fit Criterion: When a pending appointment is confirmed and the slot is still valid, 
the Appointment status changes to Confirmed and the assigned Room is recorded. 
FR-07 – Prevent schedule conflicts 
 Description: The system shall prevent double-booking of the same doctor, room, and 
timeslot. 
 Source: UC-01 Book appointment; UC-03 Manage doctor schedule; UC-04 Confirm 
appointment slot; UC-T2 Update slot availability. 
 Rationale: Avoiding conflicts is critical for operational efficiency and patient 
satisfaction. 
 Fit Criterion: When a new appointment or schedule change would overlap an existing 
confirmed Appointment for the same doctor and room, the system rejects the change and 
explains the conflict. 
FR-08 – Cancel appointment 
 Description: The system shall allow a patient or receptionist to cancel a confirmed 
appointment, subject to the cancellation policy. 
 Source: UC-06 Cancel appointment. 
 Rationale: Patients may need to reschedule and the system must free slots accordingly. 


===== PAGE 14 =====

 Fit Criterion: When a valid cancellation request is made more than 24 hours before the 
appointment time, the Appointment status is set to Cancelled, the slot is freed, and 
notifications are sent to both patient and doctor. 
FR-09 – Handle late cancellation 
 Description: The system shall enforce the cancellation policy for late cancellations (≤ 24 
hours before the appointment) by either blocking cancellation or applying 
late-cancellation rules. 
 Source: UC-06 Cancel appointment; Appointment State Machine. 
 Rationale: Late cancellations should be treated differently for fairness and revenue 
protection. 
 Fit Criterion: For cancellation requests within 24 hours of the appointment time, 
the system displays a warning and either refuses cancellation or records a CancelledLate 
status and logs any applicable penalty according to configuration. 
FR-10 – Complete treatments and appointments 
 Description: The system shall allow a doctor to mark an appointment as Completed and 
record treatment details at the end of a consultation. 
 Source: UC-05 Complete patient treatments; Appointment State Machine. 
 Rationale: Completion and documentation of treatment are required for clinical and 
billing processes. 
 Fit Criterion: When the doctor submits valid treatment details for an in-progress 
appointment, the Appointment status becomes Completed, the MedicalRecord is updated, 
and optional Prescription data is stored. 
FR-11 – Clean up expired appointments and slots 
 Description: The system shall automatically detect and update appointments and slots 
that have expired based on configured rules (e.g., no-show or time window elapsed). 
 Source: UC-12 Clean up expired slots; Appointment State Machine. 
 Rationale: Expired or unused slots must be released to keep schedules consistent. 
 Fit Criterion: When the cleanup job runs, appointments that satisfy the expiry conditions 
are updated to an Expired state and their associated slots are freed; a summary of affected 
records is produced. 
FR-12 – Generate bills for completed treatments

 Description: The system shall generate Bill records for completed treatments and 
services, either at the time of completion or as part of a periodic billing run. 
 Source: UC-05 Complete patient treatments; UC-11 Generate monthly bill; Bill State

 Rationale: Bills must be generated accurately to support payment and reporting. 
 Fit Criterion: For each completed treatment with billable items, at least one Bill is

FR-13 – Monthly billing run 
 Description: The system shall support a monthly billing process that consolidates 
relevant billable items into bills and updates their statuses. 
 Source: UC-11 Generate monthly bill. 
 Rationale: Many hospitals generate monthly statements for patients. 
 Fit Criterion: When the monthly billing job runs for a target period, it reports how many 
bills were generated or updated and the total billed amount. 
FR-14 – Make payment 
 Description: The system shall allow a patient to pay outstanding bills via an 
external payment gateway. 
 Source: UC-07 Make payment; UC-T1 Handle payment failure; Bill State Machine. 
 Rationale: Electronic payments are required for efficient revenue collection. 
 Fit Criterion: Given a bill in PendingPayment status, when the patient confirms a valid 
payment and the gateway returns success, the Bill status becomes Paid and the 
transaction is recorded. 
FR-15 – Handle payment failures and retries 
 Description: The system shall handle failed payment attempts by informing the patient, 
logging the failure, and allowing limited retries. 
 Source: UC-T1 Handle payment failure; UC-07 Make payment; Appointment and Bill 
State Machines. 
 Rationale: Robust error handling is required to deal with declined or timed-out 
transactions.

 Fit Criterion: When the payment gateway indicates failure, the system shows a clear 
error message and lets the patient either retry the payment (up to a configured maximum) 
or cancel, leaving the bill in PendingPayment. 
FR-16 – Overdue and write-off handling 
 Description: The system shall automatically mark bills as Overdue after their due date 
and allow authorized staff to mark long-overdue bills as WrittenOff. 
 Source: Bill State Machine; billing policies. 
 Rationale: Overdue tracking and write-offs are necessary for financial control. 
 Fit Criterion: After the due date passes without full payment, the Bill status changes to 
Overdue; when an authorized user chooses to write off a bill, the status becomes 
WrittenOff and the change is logged. 
FR-17 – Send appointment reminders 
 Description: The system shall send reminders for upcoming appointments to patients 
and/or doctors according to configurable lead times. 
 Source: UC-09 Send appointment reminders; Appointment State Machine. 
 Rationale: Reminders reduce no-shows and improve utilization. 
 Fit Criterion: When the reminder job runs, all appointments within the configured 
reminder window generate reminder messages; the system records which reminders were 
successfully sent. 
FR-18 – Manage notification delivery via services 
 Description: The system shall delegate delivery of reminders and notifications to an 
external NotificationService while tracking delivery success or failure. 
 Source: UC-09 Send appointment reminders; SSDs. 
 Rationale: Separation of concerns between core system and communication channels. 
 Fit Criterion: For each reminder batch, the system logs the number of successfully 
delivered and failed notifications as reported by the NotificationService. 
FR-19 – Generate weekly operational reports 
 Description: The system shall generate weekly operational reports (e.g., appointments, 
cancellations, utilization) on request from a scheduler or administrator. 
 Source: UC-10 Generate weekly report schedule. 
 Rationale: Management needs regular visibility into system usage and performance.

 Fit Criterion: When the weekly report job runs, at least one report file or summary is 
produced, or the system explicitly reports that no data is available for the period. 
FR-20 – Update slot availability 
 Description: The system shall maintain up-to-date availability of appointment slots 
based on doctor schedules, bookings, cancellations, and maintenance jobs. 
 Source: UC-T2 Update slot availability; UC-01 Book appointment; UC-03 Manage 
doctor schedule; UC-12 Clean up expired slots. 
 Rationale: Slot availability must remain consistent across all booking and scheduling 
operations. 
 Fit Criterion: After the update job runs, the number of free, reserved, and blocked slots 
matches the expected state derived from current appointments and schedules. 
FR-21 – Synchronize patient information with external systems 
 Description: The system shall synchronize selected patient data with an external patient 
information system when requested by authorized staff. 
 Source: UC-T3 Synchronize patient information. 
 Rationale: Some patient details originate from or must be shared with external systems. 
 Fit Criterion: When synchronization is triggered for a patient, data differences are 
resolved according to configured rules; on success, local Patient and MedicalRecord data 
reflect the latest agreed information or an error is reported if synchronization fails. 
FR-22 – Audit key operations 
 Description: The system shall log key operations (e.g., registration, schedule changes, 
cancellations, payments, write-offs, discharges) with timestamps and user identifiers. 
 Source: All use cases; legal and audit needs. 
 Rationale: Auditing is required for safety, accountability, and compliance. 
 Fit Criterion: For each audited operation, an entry exists in the audit log specifying the 
actor, timestamp, operation type, and affected entities.

4. Data and Domain Model 
4.1 Reference to Domain Class Diagram 
The Healthcare Management System is built around an agreed domain model derived from the 
Noun Technique and CRUD analysis in Deliverable 2. The seven core persistent domain classes 
are: 
 Patient 
 Doctor 
 Appointment 
 MedicalRecord 
 Prescription 
 Room 
 Bill 
Additional supporting classes such as Person, Staff, Nurse, Consultation, and Medicine are 
introduced in the UML Class Diagram as refinements or generalizations. The class diagram 
defines: 
 Associations between patients, doctors, appointments, rooms, medical records, 
prescriptions, and bills. 
 Multiplicities (e.g., one Patient has exactly one MedicalRecord; one Prescription 
can contain multiple Medicine items). 
 Generalization hierarchies (e.g., Patient and Staff inherit from Person; Doctor and Nurse 
specialize Staff). 
 Composition and aggregation relationships (e.g., Patient -- MedicalRecord; Prescription 
o-- Medicine). 
This SRS assumes that the implementation will be consistent with that domain model.

4.2 Glossary of Key Terms 
Term Definition 
Patient An individual receiving medical care and associated with exactly one 
MedicalRecord. 
Doctor A member of medical staff authorized to provide consultations and 
complete treatments. 
Appointment A scheduled meeting between a Patient and a Doctor at a specific date, 
time, and location. 
MedicalRecord The longitudinal clinical history for a Patient, including diagnoses, 
allergies, and notes. 
Consultation A clinical encounter between a Patient and a Doctor, typically associated 
with an Appointment. 
Prescription A set of instructions for medicines prescribed to a Patient after 
a consultation. 
Room A physical location (e.g., consultation room, ward bed) assigned to 
patients and appointments. 
Bill A financial record summarizing charges for treatments and services 
provided to a Patient. 
Reminder A notification sent to patients or doctors about upcoming appointments 
or outstanding bills. 
System scheduler An automated or administrative actor that initiates batch jobs (reminders, 
reports, billing, cleanup). 
Payment Gateway An external service that processes electronic payments initiated by the 
system. 
NotificationService An external service responsible for delivering SMS/email or other 
notifications. 
ReportingService An external service that generates operational reports from system data. 
ExternalPatientSystem An external system with which patient data may be synchronized. 
 
4.3 Business Data Model and CRUD Overview 
 


===== PAGE 21 =====

The business data model is derived from the Noun Technique, the domain class list, noun 
classification, and CRUD analysis from Deliverable 2. Core persistent domain classes include: 
 Patient – individual receiving care. 
 Doctor – medical staff conducting consultations. 
 Appointment – scheduled meeting between a Patient and a Doctor. 
 MedicalRecord – single longitudinal record per patient containing history and clinical 
notes. 
 Prescription – details of medication ordered after treatments. 
  Room – physical location assigned to patients and appointments. 
 Bill – invoice generated for services rendered. 
 
Supporting classes (Person, Staff, Nurse, Consultation, Medicine) refine and generalize these 
concepts. Key modeling decisions include: 
 Use of an abstract Person class, with Patient and Staff as specializations and Staff further 
specialized into Doctor and Nurse. 
 Composition between Patient and MedicalRecord, ensuring each patient has exactly one 
medical record. 
 Aggregation between Prescription and Medicine, allowing medicines to exist 
independently as inventory items. 
 Separation between scheduling (Appointment) and clinical work (Consultation, 
MedicalRecord). 
 
CRUD responsibilities include: 
 Patient and MedicalRecord creation and updates via UC-02 and UC-05; MedicalRecord 
is update-only. 
 Appointment creation via UC-01, updates via UC-03 and UC-04, cancellation via UC-06, 
and cleanup via UC-12. 
 Bill creation and updates in UC-05 and UC-11, and updates in UC-07 and UC-T1. 
 Deletions centralized in maintenance and cleanup processes such as UC-12. 
 
This structure ensures: 
 Each persistent class has clear CRUD ownership. 
 Use cases align with domain objects and UML diagrams. 
 Requirements FR-01 to FR-22 are supported by a stable model.

5. Non-Functional Requirements 
5.1 Look and Feel / Usability 
NFR-01 – Learnability for receptionists 
 Description: The system shall be easy for receptionists to learn for core 
tasks (registration, booking, cancellation). 
 Fit Criterion: New receptionists with basic computer literacy shall be able to 
perform registration and simple booking tasks after at most 2 hours of guided training. 
NFR-02 – Task efficiency for booking 
 Description: The system shall minimize the number of steps required for common tasks. 
 Fit Criterion: Completing a standard appointment booking (for a known patient and 
doctor) shall require no more than 7 major user interactions from start to confirmation. 
5.2 Performance 
NFR-03 – Response time for interactive operations 
 Description: The system shall provide acceptable response times for interactive 
operations (e.g., booking, cancellation, viewing bills). 
 Fit Criterion: For at least 95% of interactive requests under normal load (up to 50 
concurrent users), the system shall respond within 3 seconds. 
NFR-04 – Background job completion time 
 Description: The system shall execute batch jobs (reminders, reports, monthly 
billing, cleanup) within a reasonable time window. 
 Fit Criterion: Under normal data volumes for a medium-sized hospital, the nightly batch 
of reminder, billing, and cleanup jobs shall complete within 30 minutes. 
5.3 Operational / Environmental 
NFR-05 – System availability window 
 Description: The system shall be available during defined operational hours. 
 Fit Criterion: During scheduled operating hours (e.g., 07:00–22:00 local time), the 
system shall be available at least 99% of the time over a one-month period, excluding 
planned maintenance. 
NFR-06 – Failure handling and recovery for external services 


===== PAGE 23 =====

 Description: The system shall handle failures of external services (payment gateway, 
notification, reporting, external patient systems) without data loss or inconsistent states. 
 Fit Criterion: For simulated outages of external services, all affected requests either 
complete successfully later (retry) or are rolled back with clear error messages; no partial 
payments or orphaned appointments remain. 
5.4 Maintainability & Support 
NFR-07 – Configurable business policies 
 Description: The system shall allow key business policies (e.g., cancellation time 
window, maximum payment retries, reminder lead times) to be configured without code 
changes. 
 Fit Criterion: Authorized administrators can change these policy parameters 
through configuration or administration screens and see the new values applied to 
subsequent operations. 
NFR-08 – Logging for support 
 Description: The system shall provide structured logging of errors and key events to 
support troubleshooting. 
 Fit Criterion: For a sample of known error scenarios (e.g., payment failure, notification 
failure, invalid data), corresponding log entries exist with timestamps, user IDs (if 
applicable), and clear error messages. 
5.5 Security 
NFR-09 – User authentication 
 Description: The system shall require secure authentication for all non-public functions. 
 Fit Criterion: All access to patient, schedule, billing, and reporting functions is restricted 
to authenticated users with valid credentials; unauthenticated requests are rejected. 
NFR-10 – Role-based access control 
 Description: The system shall enforce role-based permissions (e.g., Patient, Receptionist, 
Doctor, Administrator, Finance). 
 Fit Criterion: Attempts to perform operations beyond the user’s role (for example, a 
patient trying to modify another patient’s record, or a receptionist writing off a bill 
without authorization) are denied and logged. 
NFR-11 – Data confidentiality in transit 


===== PAGE 24 =====

 Description: The system shall protect sensitive data transmitted over networks (e.g., 
login credentials, medical and billing data). 
 Fit Criterion: All application traffic that includes personal or medical data is transmitted 
over encrypted channels (e.g., HTTPS/TLS); plain-text access endpoints are disabled. 
5.6 Legal / Regulatory 
NFR-12 – Confidentiality of medical records 
 Description: The system shall protect the confidentiality of medical records in line with 
healthcare privacy principles applicable to the notional hospital. 
 Fit Criterion: Access to MedicalRecord details is restricted to authorized clinical roles; 
attempts from unauthorized roles are denied and recorded in the audit trail. 
NFR-13 – Audit trail completeness 
 Description: The system shall maintain an audit trail for key clinical and financial 
events. 
 Fit Criterion: For a sample of at least 20 random operations (e.g., bookings, 
cancellations, discharges, payments, write-offs), corresponding audit log entries exist 
with correct actor, timestamp, and operation details. 
 
6. Business Rules 
BR-01 – Appointment cancellation policy 
 Patients may cancel appointments without penalty only if the cancellation occurs 
more than 24 hours before the scheduled appointment time. 
 Cancellations at or within 24 hours of the appointment are treated as late cancellations 
and may be blocked or recorded with penalties, according to hospital policy. 
BR-02 – Single medical record per patient 
 Each patient shall have exactly one MedicalRecord that aggregates all consultations 
and clinical notes; multiple separate medical records for the same individual are not 
allowed. 
BR-03 – Bill overdue and write-off 
 A bill becomes Overdue when its due date has passed without full payment. 


===== PAGE 25 =====

 Overdue bills may later be WrittenOff by authorized staff based on hospital financial 
policy; write-offs must be logged and cannot be reversed without explicit administrative 
action. 
BR-04 – Discharge preconditions 
 A patient may be discharged only if all required treatments are completed, critical 
medical information is recorded, and no blocking conditions (e.g., mandatory 
observations) remain. This is enforced in the Authorize patient discharge use case. 
BR-05 – Payment before certain services 
 For services requiring prepayment, the associated Appointment shall not reach 
Confirmed or InProgress states until the corresponding Bill is in a Paid or acceptable 
PendingPayment state as defined by hospital policy. 
 
7. Assumptions and Dependencies 
A-01 – Unique patient identification 
Each patient has a unique identifier within the hospital system (e.g., patientID), which is used to 
link appointments, medical records, and bills. 
A-02 – Network connectivity 
The system operates in an environment with reliable network connectivity sufficient to reach 
external services (Payment Gateway, NotificationService, ReportingService, 
ExternalPatientSystem). 
A-03 – Availability of external services 
External services required by the system (e.g., payment gateway, notification, reporting, external 
patient system) provide documented and reasonably stable interfaces, and are maintained by their 
respective providers. 
A-04 – Trained users 
Receptionists, doctors, administrators, and finance staff receive basic training on the system’s 
user interface and workflows before operating it in a live environment. 
Dependencies: 
The successful operation of the Healthcare Management System depends on: 
 Deployment on suitable hardware and infrastructure (database, application server, 
network). 
 Availability of integration endpoints and credentials for external services. 


===== PAGE 26 =====

 Institutional policies that clearly define cancellation rules, billing practices, and write-off 
criteria.

9. Project Issues 
 


===== PAGE 28 =====

9.1 Open Issues 
 
The following items remain open at the SRS level but do not block design or implementation: 
• Exact user interface layout and screen design are unspecified; only behaviour and 
usability goals are defined. 
• The technology stack (database vendor, framework, deployment platform) is not fixed. 
• Hospital-specific parameters (penalties, thresholds, timing rules) are configurable rather 
than hard-coded. 
 
9.2 Risks 
 
Key risks include: 
• R-01 – Inconsistency between SRS and UML models due to misinterpretations. 
• R-02 – Underestimation of implementation effort for all use cases UC-01 to UC-12 and 
UC-T1 to UC-T3. 
• R-03 – Simplified external services in prototypes, resulting in behaviour that differs from 
real-world integrations. 
 
9.3 Waiting Room (Future Enhancements) 
 
Future enhancements, not in scope for this deliverable, include: 
• In-patient bed management, ward transfers, and admission workflows. 
• Advanced analytics dashboards (no-show prediction, capacity planning, revenue 
analytics). 
• A patient-facing mobile application with push notifications and self-service features. 
• Integration with laboratory and imaging systems for results import. 
 
9.4 Ideas for Solutions 
 
Although this SRS focuses on requirements, the following design ideas are noted: 
• Use a layered architecture separating presentation, application logic, and domain model. 


===== PAGE 29 =====

• Implement scheduled jobs for reminders, reporting, billing, and cleanup. 
• Use RESTful or message-based APIs for external integrations, consistent with SSDs. 
 
9.5 User Documentation and Training 
 
Expected documentation and training materials include: 
• A user guide explaining tasks for Patients, Receptionists, Doctors, Administrators, and 
Finance staff. 
• Short training sessions (up to 2 hours) for registration, booking, cancellation, treatments, 
and troubleshooting. 
• Reference material for administrators and IT staff on configuration options and 
monitoring background jobs.
